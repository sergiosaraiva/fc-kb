## 3.1 Some important principles

For each foreign company, the translation adjustments amount at the end of a period represents the total adjustments booked on equity and financial investment accounts to maintain them at their historical value, since the first time this company has entered the consolidation scope.

This means that during a certain number of years, not only the initial amounts but each variation of these amounts (increases/decreases of capital, shares acquisitions/disposals, transfer of result to reserves, dividends payments, ...) have been translated with so many different rates that justifying the evolution becomes rather difficult.

It is however true that the evolution of translation adjustments is in most cases required by the Auditors. To proceed further let's come back to some principles.
