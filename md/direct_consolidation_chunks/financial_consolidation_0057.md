### The exclusive control

Exclusive control is the control exercised by a company either on its own (direct control), either with one or more of its subsidiaries (indirect control). A company is considered to be a subsidiary when the parent owns more than  50%  of shares of the owned company.

An example of direct and exclusive control: Company A owns  80%  in company B.
